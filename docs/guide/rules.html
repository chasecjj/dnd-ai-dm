Advantage & Disadvantage

Advantage means roll 2d20, take the higher result. Disadvantage means take the lower. Circumstances in the game grant these — a clever plan might earn advantage, while fighting blind imposes disadvantage.

Wait for the prompt. Don't pre-roll — the DM and AI decide what rolls are needed and will ask you when it's time. Rolling without being asked won't count.

Steel and Sorcery

A beginner's guide to surviving combat

Initiative

When combat starts, everyone rolls initiative (1d20 + DEX modifier) to determine turn order. The highest rolls act first — speed and reflexes decide who strikes before the enemy can react.

Action Economy

On your turn, you have a budget of things you can do. Understanding action economy is the key to tactical play.

Action
Your main activity: attack, cast a spell, dash, dodge, disengage, help, hide, search, or use an object.
Bonus Action
Some abilities grant an extra quick action. Not everyone has one every turn.
Reaction
A response to a trigger — like an opportunity attack when an enemy runs past you.
Movement
Move up to your speed (usually 30 feet). You can split movement around actions.

HP and AC

HP (Hit Points) is your health pool. When it hits 0, you fall unconscious. Healing magic or potions can bring you back up. AC (Armor Class) is the number an attacker must meet or beat on their attack roll to hit you. Heavier armor and shields raise your AC.

Death Saves

At 0 HP, you're not dead yet. Each turn, you roll a d20:

Roll Result
10+ One success
9 or below One failure
Natural 1 Two failures
Natural 20 You spring back with 1 HP
Three successes: you stabilize. Three failures: you die.

Spell Slots

Spell slots are your magical fuel. You spend a slot to cast a spell of that level or lower. Higher-level slots can power lower-level spells, sometimes with extra effect. All spent slots recharge after a long rest.

Cantrips

Free spells you can cast endlessly. No slot required. They're your magical basics — like a fighter's sword swing, but with arcane energy. Every spellcaster knows a handful, and they scale in power as you level up.

Concentration

Some spells require concentration to maintain their effect. The rules are straightforward:

  • You can only maintain one concentration spell at a time.
  • Casting another concentration spell ends the first.
  • Taking damage might break it — you must make a CON save (DC = 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher).
  • Being incapacitated or killed ends concentration automatically.

Ritual Casting

Some spells have a ritual tag. You can cast them without spending a slot by adding 10 minutes to the casting time. This is perfect for out-of-combat utility spells like Detect Magic, Identify, or Comprehend Languages. Save your slots for when they matter.

Don't worry about memorizing spell rules. Just describe what you want to do and the AI Rules Lawyer handles the mechanics. Say "I want to cast Shield" or "I try to detect any magic in the area" and the system takes care of the rest.

Show Condition Details
  • Blinded — Can't see. Auto-fail sight checks. Attacks have disadvantage.
  • Frightened — Disadvantage on checks/attacks while the source is in sight. Can't willingly move closer.
  • Poisoned — Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.
  • Grappled — Speed becomes 0. Ends if grappler is incapacitated or you're moved out of reach.
  • Prone — Melee attacks against you have advantage. Ranged attacks have disadvantage. Must spend movement to stand.
  • Restrained — Speed 0. Attacks against you have advantage. Your attacks and DEX saves have disadvantage.
  • Stunned — Incapacitated. Can't move. Auto-fail STR/DEX saves. Attacks against you have advantage.
  • Charmed — Can't attack the charmer. The charmer has advantage on social checks against you.
  • Invisible — Can't be seen without magic/special sense. Attacks against you have disadvantage, yours have advantage.
  • Paralyzed — Incapacitated and can't move or speak. Auto-fail STR/DEX saves. Hits within 5 ft are auto-crits.
  • Petrified — Turned to stone. Incapacitated, unaware. Resistant to all damage. Immune to poison and disease.
  • Deafened — Can't hear. Auto-fail hearing checks.
  • Incapacitated — Can't take actions or reactions.
  • Unconscious — Incapacitated, prone, unaware. Attacks have advantage. Hits within 5 ft are crits.
